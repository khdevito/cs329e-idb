About Austin Food and Wine Festival

The Austin Food & Wine Festival was presented for the first time April 27â€“29, 2012, after the Texas Hill Country Wine and Food Festival, which had been held in Austin for 26 years, "transitioned" into the Austin Food & Wine Alliance[1][2][3] as a nonprofit organization.

The Austin Food and Wine Festival will benefit the nonprofit Austin Food & Wine Alliance, which is "dedicated to fostering awareness in the Central Texas culinary community through grants, educational programming and events."[2][8] The Alliance will offer three grants totaling $20,000 to Central Texas "beverage artisans, producers, culinary professionals" or to people who "represent a culinary/food-focused non-profit." They are to be awarded on December 12, 2012, at the AT&T Executive Education and Conference Center at the McCombs School of Business in Austin.[8]

Time of Year & Avg. Ticket Price

Austin Food and Wine Festival takes place at the late spring each year, generally in late April.

The "All-In" Ticket option with Tim Love Grilins is $625 and "Weekender" Ticket option with the Warrior Gospel Band is $250.
